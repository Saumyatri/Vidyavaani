OUR DATA VISUALS

Our Problem Statement:

The Goal of Quality Education for all has not been achieved because the average person does not know how they can effectively help and what resources they are offered in order to do so.

As you scroll down, you will see various data visuals we have created that focus on Asia's literacy rate, average age of students that drop out, the approximate number of schools across that allow students access to computers, etc. We hope that this will show you how serious the effects of illiteracy are.

Percentage of Schools with Computers Across India Across the Years

The graph shows a steady and significant increase in the percentage of schools
equipped with computers across different regions of India. As the
years progress, there is a notable upward trajectory, indicating a growing
recognition of the importance of integrating technology into the education system.
However, MOST schools across the nation still lack access to technology.


The Percent of Students (male and female) Who Complete Primary School

The graph shows some different countries in the Asian-Pacific Region
and focuses on the percent of children of primary school age who
complete primary schooling.


The Percent of Students (male and female) Who Complete Upper Secondary School

This graph depicts upper secondary schooling. An obvious change in the data
between the first two graphs is shown, as the rates for the completion of
upper secondary schooling are drastically lower than that of primary schooling.
This trend can be attributed to various factors, including socioeconomic disparities,
limited access to secondary education, and early workforce entry for some
students.


Total Fertility Rate by Country

The graph illustrates the fertility rates in Asian-Pacific countries,
revealing a significant trend where over half of these nations
exhibit fertility rates surpassing the
world average. The fertility rate is the average
number of children per female in a country. This
demographic trend could lead to increased population density,
putting additional pressure on resources, infrastructure, and the environment.


Fertility Rate for Select Countries

This visualization is a more concentrated version of the one above.
It focused on a select few countries and depicts the total fertility rate.


Mean Years of Schooling for Females in Select Countries

The data visualization depicts the average years of
schooling that females in each of the countries have.
Notice how the focused countries are the same
as the countries from the visualization above.
The countries with higher fertility rates tend to have
lower average years of schooling for females.
These datasets show that as women's educational opportunities
increase, fertility rates tend to decline


Gross Enrollment Ratio for Females at a High Education Level

The visualization above depicts the gross enrollment ratio
for females at a higher education level. In other words,
the percentage of females in a country who are enrolled in a
higher education institution. A conspicuous attribute of the data above is
the strikingly low rates in some of the countries.
